General FAQs

Below are some of the recently asked General FAQs.

What is rafting?

Rafting, also known as whitewater rafting, is a recreational activity that involves navigating a river or other body of water in an inflatable raft. The raft is typically guided by a trained professional or an experienced enthusiast, and can carry several people at once. The activity can range from a calm and easy float down a river to a thrilling, fast-paced ride through challenging rapids.

Rafting can be done on a variety of waterways, from gentle streams to powerful rivers with class IV and V rapids. It is a popular activity among adventure-seekers and nature enthusiasts who want to experience the excitement and beauty of the outdoors. It is also a great way to build teamwork and communication skills, as participants must work together to navigate the rapids and ensure everyone's safety.

What should I wear for rafting?

When rafting, it is important to wear appropriate clothing that will keep you comfortable and protected from the elements. Depending on the weather and water conditions, you may want to wear a wetsuit or drysuit, as well as a helmet and personal flotation device (PFD).

If you are rafting in colder water or weather, a wetsuit or drysuit will help keep you warm and prevent hypothermia. A helmet is necessary to protect your head in case of a collision or capsize, and a PFD is essential for flotation and safety. You should also wear sturdy shoes with good traction, such as water shoes or sandals with straps, to prevent slipping on rocks or in the raft. Finally, be sure to bring a change of clothes for after the trip, as you will likely get wet.

How difficult is rafting?

The difficulty level of rafting varies depending on the river and the section being rafted. We offer different trips that cater to all skill levels, from beginners to experienced rafters.

Do I need to have any prior experience to go rafting?

Prior experience is not always necessary to go rafting, as it largely depends on the type of rafting you intend to do. For leisurely rafting trips on calm rivers, no previous experience is required as the guides will provide a safety briefing and instructions on how to paddle and navigate the raft. These trips are suitable for beginners and families with children. However, for more challenging rapids and whitewater rafting, prior experience and physical fitness may be necessary. It's important to be honest with yourself and the rafting company about your skill level to ensure you are placed on an appropriate trip. Safety should always be the top priority when participating in any outdoor activity, and it's important to listen to your guides and follow their instructions.

How long does a rafting trip typically last?

The duration of a rafting trip can vary depending on a number of factors such as the length of the river, the difficulty level of the rapids, and the pace of the group. Generally, a half-day trip will last around 3-4 hours, while a full-day trip can last anywhere from 6-8 hours. Multi-day trips can range from two days to a week or more, depending on the itinerary and the length of the river.

For half-day and full-day trips, the duration will typically include safety instructions and preparation before launching, the time spent on the river, and time for changing clothes and packing up afterwards. Multi-day trips will often involve camping along the riverbank and may include additional activities such as hiking, fishing, or sightseeing. The duration of these trips will also depend on the number of stops and the amount of time spent at each one. It's important to research and plan ahead to ensure that you have an accurate idea of how long your rafting trip will take.

Is rafting safe?

Rafting can be a safe activity if proper precautions are taken and participants follow the guidelines set by the rafting company or guide. However, as with any outdoor activity, there is always some inherent risk involved.

To minimize the risk of injury or accidents, it is important to choose a reputable rafting company or guide with a good safety record. Make sure to follow all safety instructions and wear the appropriate gear, including a helmet and PFD. Be aware of the water conditions and any potential hazards, such as rocks or downed trees, and listen to your guide's instructions on how to navigate around them.

If you are a beginner or inexperienced, start with an easier trip on a calmer section of river before progressing to more challenging rapids. It is also important to know your own limits and communicate them to your guide or group. Ultimately, by taking these precautions and being aware of the risks, you can enjoy a fun and safe rafting experience.

What is the minimum age requirement for rafting?

It is important to note that age alone should not be the sole determining factor for participation in rafting trips. Other factors such as physical fitness, swimming ability, and overall health should also be taken into consideration to ensure the safety of participants. It is also important to have proper safety measures in place and to provide participants with adequate training and instruction before embarking on a rafting trip.
